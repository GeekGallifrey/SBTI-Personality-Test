## What Is SBTI? {#what-is-sbti}

**SBTI** (Silly Behavioral Type Indicator, 傻大个性格测试) is a viral Chinese internet personality test that exploded in popularity in 2026. Originally created as a humorous parody of MBTI by the Bilibili creator "蛆肉儿串儿", SBTI quickly became a cultural phenomenon across Chinese social media — and then the world.

The premise is simple and stated upfront: **"Zero science. All vibes."**

But here's the thing — despite (or because of) this radical honesty, SBTI somehow manages to hit painfully, hilariously close to home.

![try sbti test online](https://sbtipersonality.org/sbti/og-banner-v2.png)


### How It Works

SBTI evaluates you across **5 behavioral models** and **15 dimensions**, delivered through **31 straight-talking questions**. The result is one of **27 personality types** — each with its own name, character illustration, and brutally accurate tagline.

Unlike many personality tests that wrap results in flattering language, SBTI leans into absurdist humor. You might be a [Controller (CTRL)](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/CTRL), a [Joker (JOKE-R)](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/JOKE-R), or — if you're really unlucky — [Dead One (DEAD)](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/DEAD).

### The 5 SBTI Models

| Model | What It Measures |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Self** | Self-awareness, confidence, self-worth |
| **Emotion** | Emotional expression, regulation, empathy |
| **Attitude** | Outlook on life, values, openness |
| **Action** | Goal-directedness, follow-through, decisiveness |
| **Social** | Relationships, influence, social energy |

Each model covers 3 dimensions (H/M/L — High, Mid, Low), producing a 15-character pattern that maps to one of the 27 types.

## SBTI vs MBTI: What's the Difference? {#sbti-vs-mbti}

SBTI was explicitly designed as a parody of MBTI (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator). But the differences go deeper than just tone.

### Side-by-Side Comparison

| Feature | MBTI | SBTI |
|---------|------|------|
| **Origin** | Isabel Briggs Myers (1940s) | "蛆肉儿串儿", Bilibili (2026) |
| **Basis** | Jungian typology | Internet culture + behavioral observation |
| **Types** | 16 types | 27 types |
| **Dimensions** | 4 bipolar dimensions | 15 dimensions across 5 models |
| **Questions** | 93 (standard) | 31 |
| **Tone** | Serious, affirming | Brutally honest, comedic |
| **Scientific claims** | Moderate (disputed in academia) | Explicitly none — "zero science" |
| **Result style** | 4-letter code (e.g., INFJ) | Memorable acronym (e.g., CTRL, BOSS, DEAD) |
| **Character image** | None | Custom illustrated character per type |

### Why SBTI Resonates Differently

MBTI tells you you're an "Architect" or a "Protagonist." SBTI tells you you're a [Drinker (DRUNK)](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/DRUNK) or a [Swearer (FUCK)](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/FUCK).

MBTI result descriptions often feel aspirational. SBTI result descriptions feel like getting caught. The absurdist naming and the humor lower your defenses, and the dimensional profile underneath turns out to be surprisingly nuanced.

MBTI has a scientific legitimacy problem — researchers have repeatedly questioned its test-retest reliability and validity. SBTI solves this problem by having no scientific pretensions whatsoever. The honesty is part of the appeal.

## How to Take the SBTI Test {#how-to-take-the-sbti-test}

Taking the SBTI test is fast, free, and requires no sign-up.

### Step-by-Step

**1. Go to the test page**
Visit **[sbtipersonality.org/sbti-test](https://sbtipersonality.org/sbti-test)** — works on mobile and desktop.

**2. Answer 31 questions**
Each question presents a real-life scenario with multiple response options. Pick the one that sounds most like you. Don't overthink it — SBTI rewards gut reactions.

**Estimated time:** Under 5 minutes.

**3. Get your result**
You'll receive your type code, your type's name and character illustration, a tagline, and a full 15-dimension breakdown showing your scores across all 5 models.

**4. Download or share**
Your result page includes shareable cards (portrait and square format) ready for Instagram, WeChat, Weibo, or Twitter/X.

### Tips for Getting an Accurate Result

- **Answer how you actually behave** — not how you want to behave
- **Think of your default mode**, not your best-day behavior
- **Don't game it** — the types aren't ranked; there's no good or bad result
- **Retake it if you're unsure** — results can vary slightly with mood

## FAQ {#faq}

### Is SBTI scientifically valid?

No, and it says so explicitly — "zero science, all vibes." SBTI is a personality test in the tradition of internet personality quizzes, not clinical psychology instruments. It's not designed to diagnose, predict, or categorize you with any clinical accuracy. It's designed to be funny, recognizable, and shareable.

That said, the 15-dimension framework is thoughtfully constructed and many people find their results surprisingly accurate.

### Can I get a different result if I retake the test?

Yes. SBTI results can vary between sessions, especially if you're in a different mood or interpret questions differently. Unlike some rigid typology systems, slight variation is normal. Think of your result as a snapshot, not a permanent label.

### Is there a "best" or "worst" SBTI type?

No. While some types have funnier names than others ([DEAD](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/DEAD), [IMFW](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/IMFW)), no type is inherently better or worse. The humor is the point — every type has an underlying behavioral profile that reflects real ways people navigate the world.

### How many SBTI types are there?

27 types total: 25 regular types + 2 special types ([HHHH](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/HHHH) and [DRUNK](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/DRUNK)).

### Is SBTI available in English?

Yes. The test at [sbtipersonality.org/sbti-test](https://sbtipersonality.org/sbti-test) is fully available in English, with all 27 type descriptions and dimension breakdowns localized.

### How is SBTI different from other personality tests?

SBTI doesn't try to flatter you. It doesn't have 16 types named after heroic archetypes. It has 27 types including a Joker, a Swearer, and a Dead One. The combination of dimensional rigor (15 real behavioral dimensions) with deliberately absurdist naming creates a test that's both funny and strangely insightful.

### Who made SBTI?

SBTI was created by the Bilibili creator "蛆肉儿串儿" and first published in 2026. The English-language version is available at [sbtipersonality.org](https://sbtipersonality.org).

## Why Did SBTI Go Viral? {#why-did-sbti-go-viral}

SBTI hit the internet at exactly the right moment, and its viral spread wasn't an accident — it was built into the test itself.

### 1. Radical Honesty in an Age of Flattery

Every other personality test tries to make you feel special. SBTI calls you a [Joker](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/JOKE-R) or a [Waste](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/IMFW) to your face. In a media environment saturated with "you are a rare INFJ" content, blunt absurdism cuts through. When everyone's flattering you, someone calling it like it is feels like a breath of fresh air.

### 2. Group Recognition — The "That's So You" Effect

SBTI types are designed to be recognizable across social groups. When a [CTRL](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/CTRL) (Controller) shares their result, their friends immediately recognize them in the description. When someone gets [ATM-er](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/ATM-er) (Supporter), the group chat erupts. The types function as social shorthand — a vocabulary for dynamics that people already know but haven't had words for.

### 3. Shareable Content by Design

The downloadable result cards, the character illustrations, the one-line taglines — SBTI was engineered for social sharing. The visual format fits natively into Instagram Stories, Weibo posts, and WeChat Moments. The taglines are short enough to quote and strange enough to provoke curiosity.

### 4. The Power of Parody

As MBTI fatigue set in (how many "As an INTJ..." posts can one person read?), SBTI arrived as the perfect punchline. It's MBTI for people who are tired of MBTI. The format is familiar enough to understand immediately, but the content is unexpected enough to be genuinely funny.

### 5. Creator Economy Amplification

SBTI's origin on Bilibili gave it a natural creator ecosystem. Reviewers, reaction videos, friend-group testing livestreams, and "doing the SBTI test with my roommates" content proliferated rapidly. Because the test is free, fast, and produces visually shareable results, creators could incorporate it into content with minimal friction.

### 6. The Curiosity Loop

The 27 types (rather than 16) create more variation in friend groups — it's harder to have the same type as everyone else. And the unusual type names ([WOC!](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/WOC!), [MALO](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/MALO), [GOGO](https://sbtipersonality.org/personality/GOGO)) generate natural curiosity: "What even is a MALO?" The answer requires clicking, which means traffic, which means more sharing.
